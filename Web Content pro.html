Information About Sewage

Sewage is the wastewater, discharged from residence , commertial establishment, and industrial facilities.

Types of Sewage

Domestic Sewage:

This is the wastewater generated from household and appartment . It is also known as Sanitary Sewage

Industrial Sewage:

This is the wastage generated from manufacturing or chemical process. It may contain a variety of pollutant such as heavy metal, toxic chemicals, oil and grease

Storm Sewage:

Storm sewage is the runoff from the precipitation that is collected in a system of pipes or open channels.

Composition of Sewage

  • Oil and grease

Impact of Waterwastage

Water wastage can have a significant negative impact on the environment and human health if it is not properly treated.


Some of the potential impacts of Waterwastage:

Water Pollution:

Waste water can pollute rivers, lakes, oceans, and groundwater with bacteria, viruses, chemical and heavy metals. This can make the water unsafe for drinking, swimming , fishing, and other activities.

Europhication:

Waste water can contain high level of nutrients such as nitrogen and phosphorus. These nutrients can cause Europhication. Which is a process that leads to excessive algae growth in water bodies. Europhication can deplete oxygen level in water, which can kill fish and other aquatic lives.

Spread of Diseases:

Waste water can contain pathogens that can cause diseases such as cholera, typhoid and hepatitis. These disease can spread through contact with contaminated water or food.

Method TO Despose Water Waste

Sewage Treatment Plant ( STP ) :

A Sewage Treatment Plant collects wastewater and treats it to remove pollutants. The treated water then discharged into a water body such as a river or lake, or reused for irrigation or other purposes.

Septic Tank:

It is a buried, watertight tank that collects and treats wastewater from house and business. The waste water flows into the tank and separate into these layers: Solids, Scum, and effluent. The solid and scum are broken down by bacteria and effluent flows out of the tank and into a drain field, where it is further treated by the soil.

Cesspol:

It is a hole in the ground that collects and stores wastewater. Cesspol are not as effective at treating wastewater as Septic Tank, and they can pollute groundwater.

Lagoon

It is a large man-made pond that collects and treat wastewater. Lagoons are natural process such as Sunlight and algae, to break pollutants in the wastewater.

Land Application

It is a method of disposing wastewater by spraying it on land. The wastewater is treated by the soil and plants, and it can be used to irrigate crops or fertilize land.
